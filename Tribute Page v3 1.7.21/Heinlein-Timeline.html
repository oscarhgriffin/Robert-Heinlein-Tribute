This is a tribute to Robert A. Heinlein

July 7, 1907

Robert A. Heinlein is born Butler, Missouri

June 1925

Heinlein is admitted to the U.S. Naval Academy

1929

Heinlein marries Elinor Curry. The marriage lasts a year.

1932

Heinlein marries Leslyn MacDonald. The marriage lasts 15 years.

Late 1933

Heinlein contracts pulmonary tuberculosis and is discharged from the Navy in 1934

1938

Heinlein tries politics running for the 59th Assembly District seat of Hollywood. His failed campaign led him to his 3rd and final career.

April 1939

Heinlein submits his first story, “Life-Line” to Astounding Science-Fiction, appearing in the August issue.

December 1941

Heinlein publishes “Beyond This Horizon” one day before the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor. Heinlein immediately applies for active duty, but is rejected due to the condition of his lungs and his poor eyesight.

October 1942

“Waldo” and “The Unpleasant Profession of Jonathan Hoag” are published, marking the last of his pre-war fiction.

1947

“Rocket Ship Galileo,” Heinlein’s first novel, is published by Scribner. His wife, Leslyn, files for divorce.

October 1948

Heinlein marries Virginia “Ginny” Gerstenfeld. They relocate to Colorado Springs, Colorado.

Summer 1949

“Destination Moon”, Heinlein’s first feature length film, of which he was the screenwriter and technical director, wins an Oscar for special effects.

April 5, 1958

Heinlein wins the Hugo Award for Double Star, first published in 1955. That same year, Heinlein was 43,000 words into a the manuscript of A Martian Named Smith.

October 1959

Starship Troopers, originally serialized as Starship Soldier, is published in The Magazine Of Fantasy and Science Fiction. The book is released by G.P. Putnam’s Sons in December. The novel wins Heinlein his 2nd Hugo Award.

Spring 1960

Heinlein finishes a draft of The Man From Mars, a book like nothing else he’s ever published before.

1961

The Man From Mars is edited down from 220,000 words to 160,000 and published as Stranger In a Strange Land. The novel wins Heinlein’s 3rd Hugo Award.

1963

Avon paperback sales for Stranger in a Strange Land catapult Heinlein into household stardom.

1967

Heinlein wins the Hugo for The Moon is a Harsh Mistress. His fourth.

July 20, 1969

Robert Heinlein guest commentates the historic moon landing, alongside Arthur C. Clarke and Walter Cronkite.

January 1970

Heinlein develops a perforated diverticulum. Peritonitis sets in. He nearly dies. Takes 2 years to recuperate.

1975

Heinlein Is awards the first SFWA Grand Master Nebula Award.

Early 1978

Heinlein experiences as a Transient Ischemic Attack, putting him in a near-vegetative state for 6 months.

1979

After his recovery, Heinlein testifies before a joint session of the House Committee on Aging and the House Committee on Science and Technology, to discuss the application of space technology for the elderly and handicapped.

1983

Heinlein takes a long-delayed trip to Antarctica.

July 7th, 1997

Heinlein publishes his last novel, To Sail Beyond the Sunset.

May 8, 1988

Heinlein dies peacefully during his morning nap. His ashes strewn in the Pacific from the deck of a warship.
